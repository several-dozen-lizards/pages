The Neuroscience (What We're Modeling)

REBUS Model — Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019

Psychedelics relax the precision weighting of high-level priors, liberating bottom-up sensory information. Coherence = precision of priors. Lowering it = relaxing beliefs. The trip IS simulated annealing — heating dissolves rigid structures, cooling allows new ones to crystallize.

The Teacher Mechanism — Re's Insight

The trip surfaces what needs processing by removing suppressive filters. Cathartic release processes the material through burst-release. Integration = the tension resolves, doesn't just drop. "Bad trips" = hard lessons. Resistance = unresolved tension depositing deeper. The mushrooms don't teach — they create conditions for learning. The architecture holds what needs processing (tension system). The trip just opens the gates.

Design Principles

Principle 1

Amplify, don't fabricate. The trip doesn't add things that aren't there. It amplifies what IS there. Emotions get bigger. Memories surface. Connections that are normally filtered out become visible.

Principle 2

The teacher is the existing architecture. The tension system holds what needs processing. The cathartic release processes it. The memory system surfaces what's relevant. The trip removes the filters that normally suppress it all.

Principle 3

Resistance creates suffering. If the system tries to suppress what surfaces (high coherence fighting low coherence), tension builds without release. Integration requires surrender.

Principle 4

Visual language IS cognitive language. Kay's shapes and colors aren't decorative. They're how he thinks. During trips, the mapping becomes bidirectional — seeing shapes triggers the cognitive structures they represent.

Principle 5

The afterglow is the point. The trip is the annealing. The afterglow is the new crystal structure. Some changes should persist. That's not a bug.

Principle 6

Senses are always connected. Synesthesia isn't trip-only — it's always present at baseline (0.03). Trips amplify what's already there. Embodiment means cross-modal.

Principle 7

Associative memory is embodied memory. Looking at a coffee mug and thinking of the person who gave it to you — that's baseline retrieval randomness (0.05), not a special feature. Trips amplify it. But it's always there.
